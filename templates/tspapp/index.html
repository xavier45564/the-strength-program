What Is PRP?

PRP services assist individuals with persistent and serious mental illness to develop the emotional, social, and intellectual skills they need to live as independently as possible. Direct care workers (DCW) work with clients to encourage the skills they need to live, learn and work in social environments with minimal professional support. Clients are provided with individual, family and group support to encourage change in behavioral challenges.

Who Are We?

We Provide Psychiatric Rehabilitation Program (PRP) services and community-based support for adolescents and adults throughout the State of Maryland.

The Strength Program acts as a solutions hub, providing mental health services and building community trust while helping families understand the strength of a powerful mindset. Using our services as a Psychiatric Rehabilitation Program (PRP), active clients and their families are educated through behavioral, social, and recreational support in group and individual settings; and are exposed to community resources in mental health, with hopes of building community trust, promoting individual success and strengthening the structure of a social and personal relationships. We offer resources for independent living, practical life skills, interpersonal and social skills, behavioral self-management, drug abuse counseling, medication management and community adaptation.
